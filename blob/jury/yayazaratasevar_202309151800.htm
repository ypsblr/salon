Derya Yazar was born in 1971 in Erzurum, Turkey, and currently resides in Cappadocia. A multifaceted individual, she holds expertise in both Health Management and Fine Arts. As a retired nurse, she is actively engaged in various creative endeavors related to photography.

In the world of photography, she serves as a jury member in international competitions, works on social responsibility projects, and imparts her knowledge by teaching photography. As the photography delegate of Cappadocia, she plays a vital role in promoting the art form in the region.

Her passion for photography extends beyond just capturing images; she pursued a Photography and Video Master's Degree at Halic University, focusing on "The Use of Photography in the Health Sector and Its Development in the Historical Process." Her dedication to combining art with healing led her to apply "Art Therapy" using her photographs to aid patients with schizophrenia and bipolar disorder undergoing rehabilitation therapy at Nevşehir Community Mental Health Center. The positive impact of her work was recognized and acknowledged in the Best Practice Examples of Lifelong Learning in 2020 by the Ministry of National Education.

Her talent and dedication have garnered her over 300 awards and acceptances in national and international photography competitions. She has been honored with titles such as AFIAP (Artist-FIAP) in 2017 and EFIAP (Excellent-FIAP) in 2018 by the International Federation of Photographic Art (FIAP). Additionally, various photography organizations from around the world have bestowed her with fellowships and honorary titles, including Hon.FSAP, Hon.PG.NG, Hon.APF, Hon.FTPAS, Hon.PIPC, Hon.FPMP, Hon. Adv.PPI, CMP.PPI, Hon.CPE, Hon.M.GCPC, and Hon.Vedanta.

Her photographic works have been featured in numerous group exhibitions, books, and magazines, showcasing her diverse and impactful storytelling. She is an active member of various photography platforms and associations, including Altınoran Thought and Art Platform, Fotografmetre, and the Association of Women Photographers.

Throughout her career, she has hosted sixteen personal photography exhibitions, each with its unique theme and narrative. Some notable exhibitions include "Women Labourers," "From Life to Death," "Miracle Food," "Blue Colour of Freedom," "Tale of Cappadocia," "The Nature of Women," "Benin / African Children," and many others, each reflecting her deep understanding of the subjects and her artistic vision.
