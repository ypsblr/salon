Derya Yazar, born in 1971 in Erzurum, Turkey, and now residing in Cappadocia, is a versatile individual with expertise in Health Management and Fine Arts. She has a background as a retired nurse and is actively involved in photography-related creative pursuits.

In the photography realm, she serves as a jury member in international competitions, engages in social responsibility projects, and teaches photography. As Cappadocia's photography delegate, she plays a crucial role in promoting art in the region. Her dedication to combining art with healing led her to pursue a Photography and Video Master's Degree with a focus on "The Use of Photography in the Health Sector." She applied "Art Therapy" using her photos to aid patients with schizophrenia and bipolar disorder during rehabilitation at Nevşehir Community Mental Health Center, receiving recognition as a Best Practice Example of Lifelong Learning in 2020 by the Ministry of National Education.

Derya Yazar has earned over 300 awards and acceptances in national and international photography competitions. She holds titles like AFIAP and EFIAP from FIAP, and she has received fellowships and honorary titles from various photography organizations worldwide. Her photographic works have been featured in group exhibitions, books, and magazines. She actively participates in photography platforms and associations, including Altınoran Thought and Art Platform, Fotografmetre, and the Association of Women Photographers.

With sixteen personal photography exhibitions to her name, including themes like "Women Labourers," "From Life to Death," "Miracle Food," "Blue Colour of Freedom," and more, she demonstrates a deep understanding of her subjects and a strong artistic vision.
