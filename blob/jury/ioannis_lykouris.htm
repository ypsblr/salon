Ioannis Lykouris has been the Secretary General of Federation of Photographic Art (FIAP) since 2012. Prior to that he became a member of the Directory Board of FIAP in 2010 and a year later he assumed additional responsibilities for the Direction of the FIAP Biennial Service.

Ioannis Lykouris was born in Athens, Greece. He studied law at the University of Athens and accomplished his post-graduate studies in Civil and Commercial Law at the University of Paris II (Sorbonne), France. Today, he is working as a lawyer in Piraeus and he is the Deputy Legal Counselor of the Hellenic Post SA.

He took keen interest in photography at a very early age. He signed up as member of the Hellenic Photographic Society when he was just 12 years and thereby became its ever-youngest member. He became a member of the Administrative Council of the society in 1993 and assumed the role of the International Greek Liaison Officer in 1997. He has been the Vice President of the society since 2010.

He likes landscape photography, portrait, and photojournalism and creates slideshows from them with his own music. He is presently working on nude photography creating graphic shapes beyond body forms. He is widely travelled and his photographs have been published in many magazines.

His pictures have won him many awards and won him many honors from various countries. His photographs have been displayed in many exhibitions. He has also held many personal exhibitions in Greece and other countries. He has been a member of jury in many international salons.
