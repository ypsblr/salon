Hans-Peter Hornbostel, born in 1966 in Eastern Germany, has led a diverse and accomplished life, excelling in both social work and freelance journalism and photography.

His artistic journey began in the late 1980s when he became a member of the South Thuringian Literary Society, which laid the foundation for his involvement in various photography clubs and associations. From 2001 to 2017, he was an active member of the Fotoklub "Contrast" in Suhl, Thuringia, and since 2010, he has been part of the Society of Photography (GfF-Eastern-Germany).

Over the years, Hans-Peter has expanded his international photography network, becoming a member of prestigious organizations such as the German Association for Photography (DVF), "Int. Competition Photographers," and the Association of Austrian Amateur Photographers (VÖAV). He has also collaborated with various photography clubs in Austria and Serbia and earned recognition from the Photographic Society of America (PSA).

His photography predominantly focuses on landscape, travel, sports, and model subjects. Throughout his career, he has received numerous awards in both national and international competitions, with a collection of over 1100 awards, including more than 400 medals. His remarkable achievements place him consistently in the top rankings of photography competitions, including the top 10 in Germany in multiple years.

Hans-Peter Hornbostel holds esteemed honorary titles in the photography community, such as EFIAP, GMPSA, GPU VIP2, CR4, Hermes, C***Mol, R-ISF 10, GAPU, EÖVF, and GMIIWF.

Outside his photography pursuits, Hans-Peter's life has been eventful. He grew up as the fifth child in a teacher-family and faced challenges living in a restricted area near the border between East and West Germany. Despite the political climate, he actively engaged in change during the transitional period and had to navigate hurdles, including being barred from attending high school. He pursued extracurricular studies and worked in the youth welfare office for two decades.

His extensive travels have taken him to numerous countries, including Peru, Bolivia, Argentina, Society Islands, Alaska, China, Hong Kong, Thailand, Singapore, Malaysia, and many others. Throughout his journeys, he has captured captivating images of various landscapes and cultures.

Hans-Peter's life and photography journey have been marked by resilience, passion, and a deep appreciation for the beauty and diversity of the world.
