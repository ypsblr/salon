Hans-Peter Hornbostel, born in 1954, has been actively involved in photography since 1983. However, he took a slight hiatus when his children were growing up. In 2011, he reignited his passion for photography, this time embracing digital techniques. As a member and speaker of a small village club, he continuously strives to enhance his skills.

In early 2014, Hans-Peter joined DVF, which represents the German location of FIAP (International Federation of Photographic Art). Later in 2019, he ventured into international contests, broadening his horizons. He is now an esteemed member of several prestigious photography associations, including FIAP, PSA (Photographic Society of America), GPU (Global Photographic Union), MOL, and APU.

Hans-Peter's photography preferences lie in nature, particularly wildlife, although the definition of wildlife in Germany might be somewhat limited. Additionally, he finds delight in capturing architecture, the captivating moments of the blue hour, night photography, and UNESCO world heritage sites, with a special focus on Germany's cultural treasures.
