Chief Guest


Shri. Sheshadri Mokshagundam
Renowned Artist


Shri Seshadri Mokshagundam, grandson of Sir M. Visvesvaraya, is a humble and multi-talented individual. He represented international firms and managed key projects for DRDO, ISRO, and others. A connoisseur of vintage Hindi music, he has hosted TV shows, curated playlists, and is fondly known as the “Walkie Talkie of Retro Cinema.”

A lifelong artist trained under B.K.S. Varma, his works—often Renaissance reproductions—feature in global collections. He has exhibited internationally and taken on numerous commissions.

Founder of an art school in Bengaluru and the SheshadrisArtpouri page, he mentors young artists and showcases their creations through an “Artipelago” of affordable art. Sri Seshadri is celebrated not just for his art, but for inspiring the art of living.
Guest of Honour

 
Shri R Chethan, IPS
Commissioner, Dept. of Youth Empowerment and Sports, Government. of Karnataka

Shri R. Chethan, an IPS Officer of the Karnataka cadre from the 2010 batch, has garnered widespread recognition for his unwavering integrity, diligence, and effectiveness in his duties. His professional journey has been marked by diverse experiences, having served as Superintendent of Police in districts such as Chikmagalur, Ballari, Davangere, and Mysuru. Additionally, he has held the position of Superintendent of Police for the Coastal Security Police and Superintendent of Police for Anti- Naxal Force, Karnataka. He held the position of Police Commissioner for Kalburgi. 

He holds a Bachelor of Engineering degree from Coorg Institute of Technology, Ponnampet, Kodagu and a Masters in Police Management from Osmania University, Hyderabad. He also holds a PGD in Cyber Law & Cyber forensic from National Law School of India University, Bengaluru.

Presently he is serving as Commissioner, Department of Youth Empowerment and Sports.
