Theme: Heritage of the World

Exhibition Theme Definition ( PSA PIDC and FIAP Open TRAD)

“Heritage of the World” includes images that represent the heritage of the world. It can include the representation of distinctive cultures through festivals, celebrations, and ceremonies. It can also include images of distinctive, characteristic, and recognizable features of a place such as architecture, landmarks and monuments, places of worship, recognizable landscapes, cityscapes, etc.

A traditional photograph maintains the original single-capture image content with minimal adjustments which should not alter the reality of the scene and should appear natural. Rearranging, replacing, adding or removing any part of the original image except by cropping is strictly forbidden.

Content Guidelines

  • Images that misrepresent the truth, such as those from events or activities arranged specifically for photography, or of subjects directed or hired for photography, are not eligible. 
  • Close-up images of people including those that make it obvious that the image was taken in a studio setting are not allowed.
  • Images must originate as a single capture with minimal adjustments which should not alter the reality of the scene and should appear natural. However, single, long exposures (time exposures) are allowed.
  • Highly distorted images such as those produced by fisheye lenses are NOT allowed.

Allowed Editing Techniques

  • Cropping, straightening and perspective correction. 
  • Digital manipulation to fine-tune the levels and colors is allowed as long as the image looks natural.
  • Removal of dust or digital noise.

Editing Techniques that are not allowed

  • Techniques that add, relocate, replace, or remove any element of the original image, except by cropping. 
  • Complete conversion to grey scale monochrome.
  • Vignetting and partial conversion to monochrome.
  • Exposure blending (High Dynamic Range), focus-stacking and image stitching techniques, whether done in camera or post-capture, are strictly forbidden.

Borders

For the purpose of this exhibition, any border added to a digital file must be a single border of white or grey, no greater than 3-5 pixels in width.

Attention is drawn to the PSA Statement on Subject Matter which applies to all sections and to the Editing Guidelines for Heritage of the World.
